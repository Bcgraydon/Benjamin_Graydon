About Me

Benjamin Graydon

Information Technology student at UCF focused on networking, cybersecurity, and hands-on technical problem solving.

My Story

I was born and raised in Panama City, Florida, in a military family. From a young age, I was curious about how things worked and enjoyed understanding the systems behind them. Some of my proudest memories come from times that demanded hard work and resilience, such as recovering from the Category 5 hurricane that struck our town and left us without power for nearly a month.

During my senior year of high school, I enlisted in the United States Marine Corps Reserve with the goal of serving while attending college and eventually becoming an officer. My time in the Marine Corps had a lasting impact on me. It instilled in me discipline, pride in hard work, and a commitment to doing things the right way.

While preparing for an upcoming deployment, repetitive stress during training revealed a genetic condition that made it impossible for me to continue serving. Although my time in the Marine Corps ended sooner than I expected, the lessons and mindset it gave me continue to shape how I approach challenges and problem solving.

After leaving the service, I transferred to the University of Central Florida to pursue a degree that would give me a strong technical foundation. UCF’s Information Technology program stood out to me because of its practical, hands-on focus compared to many traditional programs.

What drew me most to networking and technology is how foundational they have become to modern life. Nearly everything today, from communication and infrastructure to business and national security, depends on digital systems and the networks that connect them. Understanding how those systems work, how they communicate, and how they can be secured is both challenging and rewarding, and it is what motivated me to pursue a career in the field.

Today I focus on developing skills in cybersecurity, networking, and systems analysis through both academic work and personal technical projects. I enjoy working on projects that explore how systems operate, where vulnerabilities exist, and how technology can be improved or secured.

My goal is to build a career where I can apply technical knowledge, analytical thinking, and hands-on problem solving to help design, secure, and improve the systems that modern society depends on.
