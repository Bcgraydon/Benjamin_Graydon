About Me

IT student, Marine Corps veteran, and hands-on technical problem solver.

I am a UCF Information Technology student with a Secure Computing and Networking minor, focused on entry-level IT support, network support, systems administration, and security-minded technical work.

My Story

I was born and raised in Florida and have always been interested in understanding how systems work underneath the surface. That curiosity led me toward information technology because modern organizations depend on systems that are configured correctly, maintained carefully, documented clearly, and secured properly.

During my senior year of high school, I enlisted in the United States Marine Corps Reserve. My experience as an All-Source Intelligence Analyst and Ground Sensor Operator shaped the way I approach technical work: stay disciplined, verify information, communicate clearly, maintain accurate records, and remain dependable when details matter.

At UCF, I chose Information Technology because I wanted a practical, hands-on path into technical work. I enjoy projects where I can build something real, test it, troubleshoot problems, document the process, and improve the final result. My portfolio includes Cisco Packet Tracer network designs, a laptop-based Ubuntu Server/NAS homelab, Java programming projects, and Project Hines, a long-term WWII flight logbook digitization project.

I am currently building toward roles in IT support, desktop support, network support, junior systems administration, and security-focused operations. Long term, I want to grow into technical roles where troubleshooting, infrastructure, networking, documentation, and steady execution are central to the work.

What I’m Building Toward

  • Entry-level IT support, desktop support, and help desk roles
  • Network support and junior systems administration roles
  • DoD, contractor, and security-minded IT environments
  • Hands-on work involving troubleshooting, documentation, and system reliability

How I Work

I work best when I can approach a problem methodically: identify the goal, break the system into parts, verify assumptions, test each layer, and document the final solution so someone else can understand and repeat the process.

That mindset shows up in my networking labs, Linux server work, troubleshooting notes, and long-form technical projects.
